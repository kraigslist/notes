## Required contingencies (VA assumption)

Have Troy map these to OREF forms / addenda. Plain-language intent below.

### 1. VA loan assumption approval

Sale contingent on buyer's qualification and **written approval** from the loan servicer to assume the existing VA loan at **3.490%** on terms no less favorable than disclosed (balance ~$628,000, payment ~$3,637.57/mo including escrow).

### 2. VA entitlement substitution / seller release

Contingent on servicer and VA confirming **entitlement substitution** such that seller's VA entitlement is **restored/released** and seller has **no ongoing liability** on the assumed loan after closing.

### 3. No secondary liens / encumbrances

Contingent on title showing **no undisclosed second liens**, solar UCC filings, HOA assessments, or liens that would block assumption or increase buyer's cash to close.

### 4. Inspection

Standard inspection contingency, **10–14 business days**. Pre-identify deal-breakers:

- Roof age and remaining useful life
- Cedar siding, exterior trim, water intrusion
- Crawl space moisture, insulation, pest, ventilation
- Covered deck structure and flashing
- Sewer line (scope recommended)
- HVAC documentation (listing notes new HVAC 2026)
- 1987 electrical panel and plumbing

### 5. Appraisal (if required by servicer)

If servicer orders an appraisal, contingent on appraised value **≥ purchase price** OR buyer's option to renegotiate or terminate.

### 6. Financing timeline — assumption-specific

**Closing date: 60–90 days** from mutual acceptance, extendable in **30-day increments** by mutual agreement for servicer delays. Seller to cooperate with assumption paperwork within **5 business days** of acceptance.

### 7. Loan disclosure verification

Seller to provide **current loan statement** (balance, rate, escrow, servicer contact) within **3 business days** of acceptance. Offer price and equity gap based on verified balance; if balance is materially higher than ~$628k, purchase price renegotiates or buyer may terminate.

### Do not waive

- Assumption approval
- Entitlement substitution
- Inspection
- Title / lien review
- Extended closing timeline for servicer processing

## Seller-facing narrative

Points for cover letter or listing-agent conversation:

1. **Qualified VA buyer** with entitlement for substitution — seller can restore their VA benefit.
2. **Structured assumption path** — not asking seller to carry financing or wait indefinitely without a defined timeline.
3. **Price grounded in comps** — Wahkeena $679.5k (newer, renovated); Edgebrook $663k after 152 DOM; 128 DOM with two cuts on subject.
4. **Assumable loan valued** — buyer is the pool the listing was recently marketed for (~June 9 disclosure).
5. **Cooperative timeline** — 60–90 day close with extensions acknowledges 2–4 month assumption friction.

## Negotiation playbook

### Before submitting

- [ ] Confirm assumption package status with listing agent (started? servicer name?)
- [ ] Ask about prior offers and why they failed
- [ ] Get pre-qualification letter for **VA assumption** (not generic pre-approval)
- [ ] Schedule sewer scope and inspection quickly if accepted

### If they counter at $679k

- Counter **$670k** with **$5k seller credit** toward closing (net ~$665k effective), or
- **$675k** with full assumption contingencies and no credit (ceiling)

### If they counter at $670k–$675k

- Accept if inspection is clean and servicer path is confirmed — still a win on the loan

### If they reject below $675k outright

- Wait 7–10 days, re-offer **$665k** with stronger earnest money, or hold — narrow VA buyer pool may bring seller back

### After inspection

- Use findings to justify **$5k–$15k** credit or price drop
- Cedar siding, roof, crawl space are best leverage
- Frame as deferred maintenance adjustment — monthly payment does not change

## Walk-away triggers

Terminate if any of these are true after diligence:

- Servicer won't allow entitlement substitution / seller release
- Loan balance materially exceeds ~$628k without price adjustment
- Second lien, solar lease, or title issue blocks assumption
- Inspection finds **$25k+** in near-term capex (roof + siding + sewer)
- Seller won't grant **60+ day** close for assumption processing
- Appraisal (if required) comes in **> $15k** below purchase price and seller won't adjust
